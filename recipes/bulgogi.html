Bulgogi Recipe

Description

Bulgogi, a cherished dish in Korean cuisine, showcases the art of flavor harmony. Thin slices of tender beef are marinated in a mixture of soy sauce, sesame oil, garlic, and other seasonings, creating a delectable fusion of sweet and savory. Grilled to perfection, the meat's succulent texture and caramelized edges reveal a culinary masterpiece that encapsulates the essence of Korean taste and tradition.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound / 450g thinly sliced beef (ribeye or sirloin)
  • 1 Tbsp vegetable oil (for cooking)
  • 2 Tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 Tbsp mirin (rice wine)
  • 1 Tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 1 Tbsp toasted sesame seeds
  • 2 green onions, chopped (for garnish)

Steps

  1. In a bowl, whisk together soy sauce, brown sugar, mirin, sesame oil, minced garlic, and grated ginger to create the marinade.
  2. Place the thinly sliced beef in a shallow dish or a resealable plastic bag. Pour the marinade over the beef, making sure all slices are well coated. Allow it to marinate for at least 30 minutes to an hour. For a more intense flavor, marinate longer, even overnight in the refrigerator.
  3. Heat a skillet or a grill pan over medium-high heat. Add vegetable oil to prevent sticking.
  4. Remove the beef from the marinade, letting excess marinade drip off, and place it on the hot skillet or grill pan. Cook for 2-3 minutes per side until the beef is cooked through and slightly caramelized.
  5. While cooking, you can sprinkle some of the toasted sesame seeds on the beef for extra flavor and crunch.
  6. Once cooked, transfer the Bulgogi to a serving dish and garnish with chopped green onions and additional sesame seeds
  7. Serve the Bulgogi with steamed rice and side dishes like Kimchi and pickled vegetables. You can also wrap the Bulgogi in lettuce leaves for a delicious and refreshing "ssam" experience.
